Naval Architecture & Fluid Dynamics

Designed by Unique Yacht Design and built to ABS classification, the 80-meter Leona features a highly efficient displacement steel hull. With a 12.2-meter beam and a prominent bulbous bow, her "bullet-shaped" profile was rigorously tested for hydrodynamic efficiency at the Maritime Advanced Research Centre.

Despite her massive internal volume of 1,730 GT, the hull is optimized to cut through the water with zero dynamic trim, ensuring a perfectly level and resistance-minimized ride regardless of velocity.

Propulsion Machinery

At the heart of the Leona's engine room sit twin 16-cylinder MTU 16V 4000 M73 diesel engines. Generating a combined output of 5,120 kW (over 6,800 horsepower) at 1,970 RPM, this powertrain translates sheer mechanical force into a top speed of 19 knots.

Efficiency is heavily prioritized. At her economic cruising speed of 11 knots, the engines consume just 250 liters of fuel per hour. Combined with 168,000-liter fuel tanks, this allows for an ocean-crossing range of 6,000 nautical miles. Furthermore, Leona utilizes SCR catalytic converters to feature a 100% clean air exhaust system, strictly complying with IMO Tier III environmental standards.

Acoustic & Vibration Control

In mega-yacht engineering, isolation of mechanical vibration and acoustic noise is critical. Leona achieves a "hush-smooth" passage through advanced structural damping and machinery isolation mounts, ensuring that the immense torque of the MTU engines does not transfer into the guest quarters.

For at-anchor and underway roll reduction, she relies on twin Quantum zero-speed fin stabilizers. These active hydrodynamic foils continuously adjust their angle of attack to counteract wave forces, virtually eliminating lateral roll and creating a perfectly stable platform.

Unprecedented Opulence

The interior, penned by H2 Yacht Design, is unapologetically bold. It features high-gloss lacquers, backlit onyx, and 18-carat gold inlays. The multi-deck layout prioritizes massive open spaces rather than maximizing cabin counts, offering a private cinema, a fully equipped gym, and an upper owner's deck with a private forward lounge.

The beach club serves as the yacht's crown jewel. It houses an 8.7-meter mosaic pool set beneath a fiber-optic starry night ceiling, flanked by Grecian statues and a dedicated shisha lounge, seamlessly blending the boundary between interior luxury and the open ocean.
