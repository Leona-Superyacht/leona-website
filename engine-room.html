Engine Room

The Heart of the Vessel

Twin MTU Powerplants

Superyacht engine rooms are designed to be as immaculate as the guest spaces above them, featuring gleaming white paint, chrome piping, and diamond-plate decking. At the center of Leona's machinery space sit two massive MTU 16V 4000 M73 diesel engines.

These 16-cylinder beasts generate an astonishing 2,560 kW of power each. When pushed to maximum RPM, they deliver the heavy torque required to propel the 1,730 GT steel hull to a top speed of 19 knots.

IMO Tier III Compliance

Despite her immense power, Leona is engineered for modern environmental responsibility. The engine room is equipped with a highly advanced 100% clean air exhaust system.

By utilizing Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) technology, the exhaust gases are treated before they ever leave the ship. This process drastically reduces nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions, allowing Leona to operate freely in the world's most protected and strictly regulated marine environments.

Generators & Auxiliary Systems

A floating palace requires a small power plant just to keep the lights on and the massive air conditioning systems running. Flanking the main engines are heavy-duty Caterpillar generators that supply the yacht's hotel load.

The space also houses high-capacity reverse-osmosis watermakers capable of producing thousands of liters of fresh water daily, alongside the hydraulic power packs that drive the zero-speed stabilizers, bow thrusters, and the beach club's folding sea terraces.
