Engineering & Fluid Dynamics

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

The core of Leona's performance stems from rigorous structural and fluid flow analysis. Built with a displacement steel hull and an aluminum superstructure, her center of gravity is meticulously optimized. The striking "bullet-shaped" profile isn't just aesthetic; it underwent extensive Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) testing to analyze boundary layer separation and minimize pressure drop along the hull.

The prominent bulbous bow modifies the way water flows around the hull, intentionally creating a trough that cancels out the yacht's primary bow wave. This destructive interference drastically reduces wave-making resistance, allowing the massive 1,730 GT vessel to glide efficiently with zero dynamic trim.

Structural Engineering & Materials

To balance structural rigidity with weight distribution, Leona utilizes a high-tensile steel hull mated to a lightweight aluminum superstructure. This multi-material approach lowers the overall center of mass, significantly improving transverse stability.

The structural framework is designed to withstand immense torsional loads encountered in heavy seas, while specialized isolation mounts decouple the machinery space from the living quarters, ensuring mechanical vibrations do not propagate through the decks.

Active Roll Stabilization

To combat lateral rolling forces, Leona employs Quantum zero-speed fin stabilizers. Functioning similarly to aircraft ailerons, these active hydrodynamic foils utilize sensors to detect wave motion and hydraulically adjust their angle of attack in real-time.

Underway, they generate dynamic lift to counteract roll. At anchor, they perform rapid "paddling" motions against the surrounding water, creating a highly stable platform regardless of sea conditions.
